Dr. Yashar Deldjoo
Senior Researcher & Assistant Professor, Polytechnic University of Bari

Dr. Yashar Deldjoo is a tenure-track Assistant Professor in Computer Science at the Polytechnic University of Bari, Italy, recognized among the top 2% of research scientists worldwide (Stanford’s Ioannidis database). His core expertise lies in integrating fairness, adversarial robustness, and explainability with advanced generative models, focusing on their application within Recommender Systems (RecSys) and downstream machine learning tasks. Currently, Dr. Deldjoo’s work is centered on Generative RecSys—including his KDD contributions and a forthcoming book “Recommendation with Generative Models”—investigating both the potential and pitfalls of large language models (LLMs). His research also delves into identifying risks and hazards (e.g., hallucinations, randomness, bias) while developing new evaluation metrics to ensure robust, reliable, and trustworthy AI experiences. Beyond research, Dr. Deldjoo is an Associate Editor for IEEE TKDE and ACM Computing Surveys (CSUR), and a Distinguished Reviewer for ACM TORS. He is also the Guest Editor for TORS special issues on Generative RecSys (2025) and Trustworthy RecSys (2024). He actively serves as a Senior Program Committee (SPC) member for top-tier venues including SIGIR, CIKM, ECAI, TheWebConf, and ACM RecSys.

Research

My research centers on designing and evaluating reliable and trustworthy Artificial Intelligence (AI) models, with a special emphasis on:

  • Adversarial robustness & attack defense
  • Fairness & equity in recommendations
  • Explainability & interpretability
  • Generative AI & large language models

I strive to bridge theoretical rigor with real-world applications, ensuring AI systems remain both innovative and socially responsible.
