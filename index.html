Dr. Yashar Deldjoo
Senior Researcher & Assistant Professor, Polytechnic University of Bari

His work focuses on integrating elements of trustworthy and responsible AI (e.g., fairness, adversarial robustness, security) into recommender systems, with a special emphasis on generative models. Currently, his lab’s efforts revolve around KDD'24 survey findings and a forthcoming book “Recommendation with Generative Models”. He examines the potential and hazards of large language models (LLMs)—like hallucinations, randomness, and bias—in RecSys and downstream ML tasks, developing novel evaluation metrics to ensure robust, trustworthy AI.

Research

I investigate reliable and trustworthy AI in Recommender Systems, emphasizing:

  • Adversarial robustness & attack defense
  • Fairness & bias mitigation
  • Explainability & interpretability
  • Generative AI & large language models

My aim is to bridge theoretical models and real-world deployment, ensuring AI is socially responsible.

Most Impactful Publications (Grouped by Topic)

Generative Models for RecSys

This line of work is among the most comprehensive sources to date on how generative models (VAE, Diffusion Models, GANs, text-based LLMs, multimodal approaches) can be leveraged in recommendation. It covers evaluation, risks, and hazards with fresh, updated perspectives resulting from strong academic-industry collaborations (DeepMind, Amazon, USC, UT, Poliba). These efforts significantly extend prior literature with systematically structured approaches and in-depth coverage of emerging challenges.

  • Recommendation with Generative Models (2025)
    Forthcoming book offering end-to-end coverage of Gen-RecSys theory and practice.
  • A Review of Modern Recommender Systems using Generative Models (Gen-RecSys)
    Surveys state-of-the-art generative paradigms in RecSys, from interaction-driven to multimodal systems.

Trustworthy Gen-RecSys (or downstream ML)

  • Understanding Biases in ChatGPT-based Recommender Systems: Provider Fairness, Temporal Stability, and Recency
    Offers early insights into bias assessment and temporal factors in LLM-driven recommendations.
  • CFaiRLLM: Consumer Fairness Evaluation in Large-Language Model Recommender Systems
    Proposes an evaluation framework for consumer fairness when using LLMs in recommendation.
  • Poison-RAG: Adversarial Data Poisoning Attacks on Retrieval-Augmented Generation in Recommender Systems
    Demonstrates how retrieval-augmented generative pipelines can be subverted by malicious data injections.

Trustworthy in Classical RecSys

  • Cpfair: Personalized Consumer and Producer Fairness Re-ranking for Recommender Systems
    A re-ranking solution balancing user/item-side fairness without sacrificing accuracy.
  • Fairness in Recommender Systems: Research Landscape and Future Directions
    A high-level overview of fairness definitions, metrics, and open challenges in classical RecSys.

Security & Adversarial Robustness

  • A Survey on Adversarial Recommender Systems
    Comprehensive review of attack/defense strategies, including generative adversarial networks in RecSys.

Data & Dataset Characteristics

  • How Dataset Characteristics Affect the Robustness of Collaborative Recommendation Models
    Analyzes how varied data properties influence model vulnerability and stability.
  • Explaining Recommender Systems Fairness and Accuracy through the Lens of Data Characteristics
    Examines how data distribution and sparsity relate to fairness/accuracy trade-offs.

Evaluation Frameworks (Fairness, Gen-RecSys)

  • A Flexible Framework for Evaluating User and Item Fairness in Recommender Systems
    Provides modular tools for fairness analysis across different datasets and algorithms.
  • A Unifying and General Account of Fairness Measurement in Recommender Systems
    Presents a comprehensive taxonomy and measurement protocols for fairness objectives.

Application of LLM in Downstream RecSys/ML Tasks

  • ChatGPT-HealthPrompt: Harnessing the Power of XAI in Prompt-Based Healthcare Decision Support using ChatGPT
    Pioneers the use of LLMs to provide interpretable, AI-assisted suggestions for clinical contexts.

Multimodal RecSys, Fashion AI

  • Towards Multi-Modal Conversational Information Seeking
    Explores dialogues that integrate visual and textual signals to enhance user interaction.
  • A Review of Modern Fashion Recommender Systems
    Extensive survey bridging computer vision, personalization, and user modeling in fashion domains.
