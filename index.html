Dr. Yashar Deldjoo
Senior Researcher & Assistant Professor, Polytechnic University of Bari

Dr. Yashar Deldjoo is a tenure-track Assistant Professor in Computer Science at the Polytechnic University of Bari, Italy, recognized among the top 2% of research scientists worldwide (Stanford’s Ioannidis database). His expertise centers on integrating fairness, adversarial robustness, and explainability with generative models for Recommender Systems (RecSys) and downstream ML applications.

Currently, Dr. Deldjoo’s work focuses on Generative RecSys—including KDD contributions and a forthcoming book “Recommendation with Generative Models”—while investigating both the potential and hazards of large language models (LLMs) (e.g., hallucinations, randomness, bias). To address these risks, he develops novel evaluation metrics and methods to ensure robust, trustworthy AI experiences.

He has recently shared insights into Generative RecSys on the Recsperts Podcast, discussing the ethical and technical challenges of next-generation recommender systems. Beyond research, Dr. Deldjoo is an Associate Editor for IEEE TKDE and ACM Computing Surveys (CSUR), a Distinguished Reviewer for ACM TORS, and a Guest Editor for TORS special issues on Generative RecSys (2025) and Trustworthy RecSys (2024). He actively serves as a Senior Program Committee (SPC) member for top-tier venues, including SIGIR, CIKM, ECAI, TheWebConf, and ACM RecSys.

Research

My research investigates the development of reliable and trustworthy AI in Recommender Systems, emphasizing:

  • Adversarial robustness & attack defense
  • Fairness & equity in recommendations
  • Explainability & interpretability
  • Generative AI & large language models

I combine theoretical models with real-world experimentation to ensure AI systems remain both pioneering and societally responsible.
