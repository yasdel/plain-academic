About Me

I am Yashar Deldjoo, a dedicated research scientist and tenure-track Assistant Professor at the Polytechnic University of Bari. With a Ph.D. from Politecnico di Milano and an M.Sc. from Chalmers University of Technology, my career is driven by the quest for innovative solutions in recommender systems and trustworthy artificial intelligence.

In my work, I combine rigorous quantitative analysis with an emphasis on fairness, robustness, and explainability. I have served as Associate Editor for IEEE TKDE and ACM Computing Surveys (CSUR), and I am a Distinguished Reviewer for ACM Transaction on Recommender Systems (TORS). I am currently guest editing two special issues: one on Generative Recommender Systems (2025) and another on Trustworthy Recommender Systems (2024).

My research contributions include influential surveys, technical advances, and practical tutorials that have shaped the development of recommender systems across diverse domains. I remain committed to advancing the academic and practical understanding of personalized, fair, and explainable recommendation models.

Research Interests

My work focuses on developing recommender systems that integrate large language models and generative techniques while ensuring fairness, robustness, and transparency. I investigate how advanced AI models can be harnessed to improve personalization without compromising ethical standards. My research spans:

  • Trustworthy Recommender Systems
  • Generative AI and Large Language Models for Personalization
  • Fairness, Robustness, and Explainability in Machine Learning
  • Interactive and Adaptive User Interfaces
