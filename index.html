Dr. Yashar Deldjoo
Senior Researcher & Assistant Professor, Polytechnic University of Bari

Dr. Yashar Deldjoo is a senior research scientist and a tenure-track Assistant Professor in Computer Science at the Polytechnic University of Bari, Italy. He is recognized among the top 2% of research scientists worldwide in 2023–2024 (Stanford’s Ioannidis database). His expertise centers on integrating trustworthy and responsible AI principles (e.g., fairness, adversarial robustness, security) into recommender systems, with a strong emphasis on generative models. Currently, his lab focuses on Recommendation with Generative Models (Gen-RecSys)—see, for instance, the KDD’24 survey and the forthcoming book “Recommendation with Generative Models”. He investigates both the potential and hazards of large language models (LLMs)—like hallucinations, randomness, and bias—in downstream RecSys and ML tasks, developing novel evaluation metrics to ensure robust, trustworthy AI experiences. He recently shared insights on Generative RecSys in a Recsperts Podcast, discussing the ethical and technical challenges of next-generation recommender systems. Dr. Deldjoo is an Associate Editor for IEEE TKDE and ACM Computing Surveys (CSUR), a Distinguished Reviewer for ACM TORS, and a Guest Editor for TORS special issues on Generative RecSys (2025) and Trustworthy RecSys (2024). He also serves as a Senior Program Committee (SPC) member for top-tier venues including SIGIR, CIKM, ECAI, TheWebConf, and ACM RecSys.

Research

I focus on designing reliable and trustworthy AI for Recommender Systems, emphasizing:

  • Adversarial robustness & attack defense
  • Fairness & equity in recommendations
  • Explainability & interpretability
  • Generative AI & LLMs

My goal is to merge theoretical developments with real-world experimentation for advanced, socially responsible AI.
