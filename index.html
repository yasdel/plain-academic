Dr. Yashar Deldjoo
Senior Researcher & Assistant Professor, Polytechnic University of Bari

Dr. Yashar Deldjoo is a senior research scientist and a tenure-track Assistant Professor in Computer Science at the Polytechnic University of Bari, Italy, recognized among the top 2% of research scientists worldwide (Stanford’s Ioannidis database, 2023–2024). His work focuses on integrating elements of trustworthy and responsible AI (e.g., fairness, adversarial robustness, security) into recommender systems. Currently, his lab’s efforts revolve around the potential and hazards of large language models (LLMs)—like hallucinations, randomness, and bias—in RecSys and downstream ML tasks, developing novel evaluation metrics to ensure robust, trustworthy AI. Dr. Deldjoo recently presented next-generation RecSys in a forthcoming book “Recommendation with Generative Models”, and the KDD'24 survey—a multidisciplinary collaboration involving major companies (Amazon, DeepMind) and universities. He recently discussed next-generation RecSys with the Recsperts Podcast. Another line of research he has been spearheading concerns advancements in multimodal learning for RecSys, Fashion AI, and the music industry, with numerous contributions in these areas.

Dr. Deldjoo is Associate Editor for IEEE TKDE and ACM CSUR, Distinguished Reviewer for ACM TORS, and Guest Editor for TORS special issues on Generative RecSys (2025) and Trustworthy RecSys (2024). He also serves as a Senior Program Committee (SPC) member for top-tier venues including SIGIR, CIKM, ECAI, TheWebConf, and ACM RecSys. Short version: Recognized among top 2% of scientists, Dr. Deldjoo bridges fairness, robustness, and security with generative approaches in Recommender Systems. He focuses on LLM hazards (e.g., hallucinations), ethical metrics, and real-world impacts through academic-industry collaborations.

Research

I investigate reliable and trustworthy AI in Recommender Systems, emphasizing:

  • Adversarial robustness & attack defense
  • Fairness & bias mitigation
  • Explainability & interpretability
  • Generative AI & large language models

My aim is to bridge theoretical models and real-world deployment, ensuring AI is socially responsible.
