Dr. Yashar Deldjoo
Senior Researcher & Assistant Professor, Polytechnic University of Bari

Dr. Yashar Deldjoo is a tenure-track Assistant Professor at the Polytechnic University of Bari, Italy, and a senior research scientist specializing in Trustworthy AI and Generative Recommender Systems (GenRecSys). Currently, his research is highly focused on the application of generative models within recommender systems and other downstream machine learning applications, including the development of evaluation metrics for AI systems. He is particularly interested in identifying and mitigating the risks and hazards associated with these models, including hallucination, randomness, and bias. His recent work has been featured in major venues, including KDD and ACM CSUR, as well as his forthcoming book, "Recommendation with Generative Models", scheduled for publication in 2024–2025.

Dr. Deldjoo currently serves as an Associate Editor for IEEE TKDE and ACM Computing Surveys (CSUR), and is a Distinguished Reviewer for ACM Transactions on Recommender Systems (TORS). He is also a Senior Program Committee (SPC) member for top-tier conferences such as SIGIR, CIKM, WebConf, and ECAI. His research interests cover generative models, fairness, adversarial robustness, explainability, and the broader impacts of AI systems.

Research

My research centers on the intersection of Generative Models, Recommender Systems, and Trustworthy AI, with a focus on:

  • Development of trustworthy AI systems for recommender applications
  • Building robust evaluation metrics for generative models in ML
  • Understanding the risks of generative models: hallucination, randomness, and bias
  • Integrating fairness, adversarial robustness, and explainability into AI systems

My work seeks to create models that are not only innovative but also safe, explainable, and fair, particularly in the domain of recommender systems.
