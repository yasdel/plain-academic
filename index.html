About Me

I am a tenure-track Assistant Professor at the Polytechnic University of Bari, Italy, and a senior research scientist working at the intersection of Trustworthy AI and Generative Recommender Systems (GenRecSys). My research focuses on embedding crucial AI principles—fairness, adversarial robustness, and explainability—into next-generation recommender systems. In 2023 and 2024, I was recognized among the top 2% of research scientists worldwide (Stanford University's Ioannidis database).

Currently, I serve as an Associate Editor for IEEE TKDE and ACM Computing Surveys (CSUR), and I am a Distinguished Reviewer at ACM Transactions on Recommender Systems (TORS). I am also the Guest Editor for two TORS Special Issues on Generative RecSys (2025) and Trustworthy RecSys (2024). My recent projects investigate the opportunities and risks posed by large language models (LLMs) in recommendation scenarios, exploring how generative technology can shape future personalization methods and address societal concerns.
