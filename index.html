Dr. Yashar Deldjoo
Senior Researcher & Assistant Professor, Polytechnic University of Bari

Dr. Yashar Deldjoo is a senior research scientist and a tenure-track Assistant Professor in Computer Science at the Polytechnic University of Bari, Italy, recognized among the top 2% of research scientists worldwide (Stanford’s Ioannidis database, 2023–2024). His work focuses on integrating elements of trustworthy and responsible AI (e.g., fairness, adversarial robustness, security) into recommender systems, with a special emphasis on generative models. Currently, his lab’s efforts revolve around KDD'24 survey findings and a forthcoming book “Recommendation with Generative Models”. He examines the potential and hazards of large language models (LLMs)—like hallucinations, randomness, and bias—in RecSys and downstream ML tasks, developing novel evaluation metrics to ensure robust, trustworthy AI. Dr. Deldjoo recently discussed next-generation RecSys on the Recsperts Podcast. He is Associate Editor for IEEE TKDE and ACM CSUR, Distinguished Reviewer for ACM TORS, and Guest Editor for TORS special issues on Generative RecSys (2025) and Trustworthy RecSys (2024). He also serves as a Senior Program Committee (SPC) member for top-tier venues including SIGIR, CIKM, ECAI, TheWebConf, and ACM RecSys.

Research

I investigate reliable and trustworthy AI in Recommender Systems, emphasizing:

  • Adversarial robustness & attack defense
  • Fairness & equity in recommendations
  • Explainability & interpretability
  • Generative AI & large language models

My aim is to link theoretical models and real-world deployment, ensuring AI is both innovative and socially responsible.
